3-Day La Union Trip

Expense audit and final settlement plan — who owes what, why, and how it gets squared with the fewest possible payments.

July 10–13 6 travelers ₱16,174.81 total spend 5 payments to settle

01

Purpose

This audit reconciles all six people's tabs — the master "all" sheet plus the four individual payer tabs (Rica, Juls, Gio, and Richard) — into a single, minimal set of payments that settles everyone up. Instead of everyone chasing everyone else, each person makes at most one or two payments and the group is even.

02

How the numbers were derived

  • Each of the four payer tabs (Rica, Juls, Gio, Richard) lists the items that person fronted ("Nag Abono") and a breakdown of how each item's cost was split among the group.
  • For each tab, every other person's column total is the amount they owe that payer. The payer's own column is their personal share — not a debt to anyone.
  • Adding up what each person owes across all four tabs, and what each payer is owed in total, gives everyone a single net position: positive means the group owes them money, negative means they still owe the group.
  • The settlement plan matches the largest debtors to the largest creditors first, so debts cancel out in as few transactions as possible.

03

Data issues found during the audit

Two real gaps on the Juls tab were caught and corrected before finalizing:

Flotsam (₱925) was never split. The description row says it should be divided 6 ways (₱154.17 each), but the breakdown row was left completely blank. It's added back into every total below — it's a real, documented cost.
The tab's own "Total" row doesn't match its line items. After Top Load and Tourguide were added, the total formula wasn't updated consistently across columns — only Rica's total reflected the new rows. The figures below were recomputed directly from line items and cross-checked against total trip spend.
Still open: "Coffee" and "Kaha" on the Juls tab have no peso amount recorded, so they're excluded from every total below. Rica's own tab also has a small, unexplained ₱77 gap between its line items and its stated total — worth a quick check, though it won't meaningfully change the settlement plan.

Why this is the minimum: with 4 people owing money (Honey, Jorge, Gio, Richard) and 2 people owed money (Juls, Rica), the fewest possible transactions to settle everyone is (debtors + creditors − 1) = 5 — exactly what's listed above. No further reduction is mathematically possible without a payment that doesn't correspond to a real debt.
